概要 · OVERVIEW

Kansai's loudest, friendliest city

Four nights of takoyaki steam, river reflections in Dōtonbori, and a great jumping-off point for Kyoto's temples and Nara's bowing deer.

Osaka is the eating city — takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu, kitsune udon — and it leans into it. Stay near Namba or Umeda and you're a short train ride from Kyoto's old streets, Nara's deer park, or Himeji Castle. Kansai in December means winter illuminations and a whole lot of warm bowls.

見所 · HIGHLIGHTS

What we're looking forward to

🐙

Eating our way through Dōtonbori

Takoyaki from a street window, okonomiyaki cooked on the table, kushikatsu in a standing bar, and matcha soft-serve somewhere in between.

🎐

Shinsekai & Namba at night

Glittering signs, the giant running Glico man, narrow streets of lantern-lit izakaya — Osaka at its most cinematic.

⛩️

Kyoto day trip

Fushimi Inari's endless vermilion torii at sunrise, Arashiyama bamboo, a quiet temple garden, and a matcha and wagashi break in Gion.

🦌

Nara's deer park

Hundreds of very polite (and slightly pushy) sika deer that bow for crackers, plus the enormous Tōdai-ji Buddha on the same walk.

🏯

Himeji Castle

A day trip west to Japan's most beautiful original castle — a white heron against a winter sky.

☕

Coffee & kissaten

Osaka has a wonderful third-wave coffee scene and quiet old kissaten with pudding and thick toast — perfect cold-weather stops.
